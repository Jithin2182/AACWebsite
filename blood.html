Detect Blood Loss Using Image Processing

Problem Statement:

Postpartum hemorrhage is excessive bleeding following the birth of a baby. About 1 to 5 percent of women have postpartum hemorrhage and it is more likely with a cesarean birth. Hemorrhage most commonly occurs after the placenta is delivered. The average amount of blood loss after the birth of a single baby in vaginal delivery is about 500 ml (or about a half of a quart). The average amount of blood loss for a cesarean birth is approximately 1,000 ml (or one quart). Most postpartum hemorrhage occurs right after delivery, but it can occur later as well. Replacing lost blood and fluids is important in treating postpartum hemorrhage. Intravenous (IV) fluids, blood, and blood products may be given rapidly to prevent shock. Postpartum hemorrhage can be quite serious. However, quickly detecting and treating the cause of bleeding can often lead to a full recovery.

The main aim of this project is to calculate exact blood loss during pregnancy if we detect the exact blood loss then this problem can be easily resolved.

Expected Outcome:

Main aim is to measure the surgical patient’s current blood loss and blood loss rate. It is likely that we have to run millions of images through developed algorithm showing surgical sponges in various states of bloodiness. This would have trained the algorithm to discern the sequences and patterns of 1’s and 0’s that, to the human eye, form the image of sponge soaked in a various amount of blood.

This would then allow a surgeon to hold up a bloody sponge to a camera, and software would, in theory, determine how much blood is on the sponge. This information could then be used to determine how much blood the surgical patient lost prior to or during the surgery. The estimated blood loss is displayed on the mounted device for the physician to see.
